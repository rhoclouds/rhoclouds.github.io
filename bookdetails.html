    all time favorites

  • The Great Gatsby by F. Scott Fitzgerald

    • in my eyes, this is *the* great american novel. the rich and the richest collide in grandiose mansion parties and on the streets of a roaring twenties era new york, providing commentary on love, materialism, and the flawed ideals of the american dream. to put it simply: champagne problems with some of the most ornate and magnificent prose you'll read.

  • Invisible Man by Ralph Ellison

    • this serves as one of, if not the most influential pieces of african-american literature ever. an existentialist, disquieting, and murky masterpiece that takes the reader from the likes of a hellscape capitalist paint factory to riots on the streets of harlem. its words live on to this day, through both its influence and message.

  • The Selected Poetry of Rainer Maria Rilke

    • is this the greatest poetry collection ever? perhaps. it's difficult to put into words how complex, transcendent, and inspirational his poetry is.

  • After Dark by Haruki Murakami

    • surrealism and beautiful loneliness are common for murakami, but this is different. a "one night stand" into the world of nocturnal tokyo that is perfectly encapsulated by by its opening scene: spending the night in a diner, running into someone you used to know, and then diving into an offbeat city of neon with them.
